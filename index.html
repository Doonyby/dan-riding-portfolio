About Me

Hey there, I'm Dan, a former AODA Case Manager with a degree in Wilderness Rescue, turned full stack web developer.

I love spending time with fun, adventurous people, both outdoors and in, but was frustrated that most mainstream social media channels divided, rather than drew people together. I wanted technology that drew us into exciting pursuits that we could share with one another instead of isolating us. So I set out to master the latest web development methods in order to fulfill my mission to create technology that helps people connect with the outdoors, and with each other.

Projects

Inside Book Club

A more thoughtful social network for the bookworm in you.


Isn't a book club best as a smaller group of people that can enjoy one another's company and talk about books together? Inside Book Club is a way to accomplish this online and provide a space to host these clubs remotely.

Inside Book Club was designed with a more exclusive approach to social media. It helps people connect on a smaller scale and bypass comment feeds loaded with tedious opinions from people you don't care about.

Inside Book Club was built using Node.js, Express, MongoDB, React.js and Redux.

Back Country Ski Buddy

A meetup designed for the powder fiends of the Utah back country.


Back Country Ski Buddy allows a user to connect with other people in their community with skill levels appropriate for their planned outings.

Traveling alone in the back country can be extremely dangerous due to avalanche hazards. This app promotes safe practices by facilitating touring companionships.

This app was built using Node.js, Express, MongoDB, and Jquery.

Map My Elevation

An app designed to better plan your exercise route.


Map My Elevation was originally created for the road biking community, but can be used by runners, motorists, or any other individual who is curious about elevation during a specific route.

A user can quickly type in starting and ending points to see what kind of ride they are in for and plan accordingly.

Map My Elevation uses Google Maps API as well as Jquery and Ajax.
